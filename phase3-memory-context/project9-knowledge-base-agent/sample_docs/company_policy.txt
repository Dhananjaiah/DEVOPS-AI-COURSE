TechCorp Human Resources Policy Manual
Version 3.2 | Last Updated: January 2026

SECTION 1: EMPLOYMENT POLICIES

1.1 Equal Opportunity Employment
TechCorp is an equal opportunity employer. We are committed to providing a work environment free from discrimination and harassment. Employment decisions are based solely on qualifications, merit, and business need. TechCorp does not discriminate on the basis of race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, disability, genetic information, veteran status, or any other legally protected characteristic.

1.2 Work Hours and Schedules
Standard work hours are 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM, Monday through Friday. TechCorp operates on a flexible work arrangement policy. Employees may request flexible scheduling, remote work, or compressed work weeks with manager approval. Core collaboration hours are 10:00 AM to 3:00 PM, during which all team members should be available for meetings and collaboration. Overtime is compensated at 1.5x the regular hourly rate for non-exempt employees.

1.3 Remote Work Policy
Full-time remote work is available for eligible roles. Employees in remote positions must maintain a dedicated workspace, have reliable high-speed internet (minimum 50 Mbps download), and be available during core hours. Remote employees are required to attend in-person team meetings at least once per quarter. Equipment for remote workers (laptop, monitor, keyboard) is provided by TechCorp and remains company property.

SECTION 2: COMPENSATION AND BENEFITS

2.1 Salary Review
Performance reviews are conducted annually in December. Salary adjustments take effect on January 1st of the following year. Merit increases are based on performance ratings: Exceptional (5-8% increase), Meets Expectations (2-4% increase), Needs Improvement (0% increase). Promotions may include salary increases outside of the annual cycle.

2.2 Health Insurance
TechCorp provides comprehensive health insurance for all full-time employees and their eligible dependents. Coverage includes medical, dental, and vision plans. The company covers 80% of the employee premium and 60% of dependent premiums. Open enrollment occurs each October for coverage effective January 1st. New employees are eligible for coverage beginning on the first day of the month following their hire date.

2.3 Retirement Benefits
TechCorp offers a 401(k) retirement savings plan. The company matches 100% of employee contributions up to 4% of salary. Employees are eligible to participate after 90 days of employment. The company match vests over a two-year period: 50% vested after one year, 100% vested after two years. Employees can choose from a variety of investment options including index funds, target-date funds, and ESG options.

2.4 Paid Time Off
Full-time employees receive 15 days of paid vacation per year, increasing to 20 days after 3 years and 25 days after 7 years. TechCorp observes 11 federal holidays annually. Employees receive 7 sick days per year. Unused vacation can be carried over (maximum 5 days). Unused sick days do not carry over but are paid out at 50% upon termination.

SECTION 3: PROFESSIONAL DEVELOPMENT

3.1 Learning and Development Budget
Each employee receives an annual professional development budget of $2,000. This can be used for courses, certifications, conferences, or books related to their role. Requests must be submitted to HR at least 2 weeks before the expense. Pre-approved expenses are reimbursed within 30 days of receipt submission. TechCorp also provides access to LinkedIn Learning and O'Reilly Online Learning at no cost.

3.2 Mentorship Program
TechCorp operates a formal mentorship program pairing junior employees with senior staff. Participation is voluntary but strongly encouraged. Mentor-mentee pairs meet for at least one hour per month. The program runs on a 12-month cycle starting each January. Mentors receive 2 additional days of PTO annually in recognition of their contribution.

SECTION 4: CODE OF CONDUCT

4.1 Confidentiality
All employees must maintain strict confidentiality regarding TechCorp's proprietary information, customer data, and business strategies. Employees sign a Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) upon hiring. Confidentiality obligations continue for 2 years after employment ends. Unauthorized disclosure of confidential information may result in legal action and immediate termination.

4.2 Conflict of Interest
Employees must disclose any outside employment, investments, or relationships that may conflict with TechCorp's interests. Approval from the Ethics Committee is required before engaging in outside work for a competitor or client. Board membership in other organizations requires VP-level approval.

4.3 Social Media Policy
Employees may use social media but must not share confidential information, disparage TechCorp or its customers, or represent opinions as company positions without authorization. TechCorp's social media accounts are managed exclusively by the Marketing team. Employees identifying as TechCorp staff on personal social media should include a disclaimer that views are their own.

SECTION 5: DISCIPLINARY PROCEDURES

Progressive discipline is used for policy violations: verbal warning, written warning, final written warning, and termination. Serious violations (theft, harassment, gross misconduct) may result in immediate termination. All disciplinary actions are documented in the employee's personnel file. Employees have the right to respond to any disciplinary action in writing within 5 business days.
